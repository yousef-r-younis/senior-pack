What is IEEE Citation Style?

IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) style uses numbered references in square brackets [1], [2], [3] that appear in the order they are first cited in your text. The full reference list appears at the end of your report.

üí° Key Rule

References are numbered in the order they first appear in your text ‚Äî NOT alphabetically.

In-Text Citations

Basic Rules:

  • Use numbers in square brackets: [1], [2], [3]
  • Place the citation before the period at the end of the sentence
  • Multiple citations: [1], [3] or [1]‚Äì[3] for a range
  • Do NOT use the author's name as the subject of a sentence

Examples:

‚úÖ Correct:

"The performance of OpenWrt-based routers has been studied extensively [1]."

‚úÖ Correct:

"Recent studies [2], [5] have shown improvements in network throughput."

‚úÖ Correct (with author):

"As demonstrated by Smith et al. [3], the latency was reduced by 40%."

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes:

  • ‚ùå "In [1], Smith found that..." ‚Üí Use: "Smith [1] found that..."
  • ‚ùå "Reference 1 states..." ‚Üí Use: "As stated in [1]..."
  • ‚ùå Placing citation after the period. ‚Üí Always before the period

Reference List Formats

Each source type has a specific format. Here are the most common ones you'll need:

1. Journal Article

[#] A. A. Author, "Title of article," Title of Journal, vol. X, no. X, pp. XX‚ÄìXX, Month Year.
Example:

[1] J. K. Author, "Name of paper," IEEE Trans. Aerosp. Electron. Syst., vol. 28, no. 3, pp. 263‚Äì271, Jul. 1992.

2. Conference Paper

[#] A. A. Author, "Title of paper," in Proc. Conference Name, City, Country, Year, pp. XX‚ÄìXX.
Example:

[2] D. B. Payne and H. G. Gunhold, "Digital sundials and broadband communications," in Proc. IEEE Int. Conf. Commun., Ottawa, Canada, 2004, pp. 109‚Äì114.

3. Book

[#] A. A. Author, Title of Book, xth ed. City, Country: Publisher, Year.
Example:

[3] B. Klaus and P. Horn, Robot Vision. Cambridge, MA, USA: MIT Press, 1986.

4. Website / Online Source

[#] A. A. Author, "Title of page," Website Name, Year. [Online]. Available: URL. [Accessed: Month Day, Year].
Example:

[4] OpenWrt Project, "Documentation," 2024. [Online]. Available: https://openwrt.org/docs. [Accessed: Jan. 15, 2025].

5. Technical Report / Datasheet

[#] A. A. Author, "Title of report," Company/Organization, City, Country, Rep. Number, Year.
Example:

[5] Raspberry Pi Foundation, "Raspberry Pi 4 Model B datasheet," Cambridge, UK, 2019.

6. Thesis / Dissertation

[#] A. A. Author, "Title of thesis," M.S./Ph.D. thesis, Dept., Univ., City, Country, Year.
Example:

[6] N. Kawasaki, "Parametric study of thermal and chemical nonequilibrium nozzle flow," M.S. thesis, Dept. Electron. Eng., Osaka Univ., Osaka, Japan, 1993.

What Must You Cite?

‚úÖ Always Cite:
  • Research papers you referenced or built upon
  • Technical specifications and datasheets
  • Statistics or data from external sources
  • Algorithms you didn't invent
  • Open-source libraries and frameworks used
  • Official documentation you referenced
  • Standards (IEEE, RFC, ISO)

‚ö†Ô∏è You Do NOT Need to Cite:

  • Common knowledge ("TCP uses a three-way handshake")
  • Your own original work and findings
  • Basic definitions widely known in your field

Pro Tips for IEEE Citations

üí° Abbreviate Journal Names

IEEE journals have standard abbreviations. E.g., "IEEE Transactions on Aerospace and Electronic Systems" ‚Üí IEEE Trans. Aerosp. Electron. Syst.

üí° Use "et al." for Many Authors

For more than 6 authors, list the first author followed by "et al." E.g., "J. Smith et al."

üí° Keep Track as You Go

Save citation info every time you read a paper or use a resource. It's much harder to find this info later. Use a spreadsheet or reference manager to stay organized.

üí° Aim for 10-20 References

A good senior project report typically has 10-20 quality references. Quality matters more than quantity ‚Äî make sure each reference is relevant.
